About Me

Bio

I'm a recent graduate from the University of Washington that majored in Informatics. I began programming as a hobby when I was a teenager in middle school, making small console applications in C++. In high school I took my first programming classes and joined my school's FIRST FRC robotics team. By this point in my life I knew I wanted to do software develpoment as a career. I got accepted to the University of Washington (UW) and decided to pursue this through studying computer science, however, I was unable to get into the program because of how competitive it was. After applying twice, I pursued both switching majors and transferring to another university. It was a difficult time, however, I ended up getting accepted into the Informatics program and decided to pursue that degree instead of transferring. I continued to take programming classes within my major and continued to improve my skills. I believe my time was well spent in that program, despite that, I have been having a difficult time starting my career. Several personal factors, including being socially isolated due to the pandemic and low self-confidence, have made this transition from school to career especially difficult for me. After revising and editing my resume many times and seeking out advice, I decided to enroll in Epicodus and am feeling hopeful about this new direction.
